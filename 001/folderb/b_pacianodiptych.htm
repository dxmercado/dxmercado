REDRAWING PACIANO & PACIANO REDRAWN

by MakiSining (Posted on 2014 May 05)






Redrawing Paciano and Paciano Redrawn by Doel Salvador R. Mercado are a diptych -- one acrylic on canvas and another one coffee on canvas -- is a physical metaphor on the academic process of learning about a rather unmentioned historical figure. Redrawing Paciano features a faint visage, vanishing in sepia tones, illustrating how it feels like to tey and remember a memory -- it all feels familiar, yet so distant. And then when all the details are had and learned, it all comes together with bold, concrete lines, forming a distinct face and producing only identifiable detail now perceived with new perspective -- Paciano is redrawn. Redrawing Paciano and Paciano Redrawn now belong to the private collection of the descendants of Rizal. The paintings are hung at the home of Paciano Rizal.
